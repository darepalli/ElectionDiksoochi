About India Election Compass

What this civic tool does and why it exists

Home About Methodology & Sources GitHub

Purpose

India Election Compass is a non-partisan civic information project that helps voters compare their policy preferences with publicly stated positions of parties, alliances, or candidates.

It is designed to keep voter attention on policy choices and evidence-backed positions, while reducing noise from high-intensity campaign narratives.

The goal is to make policy comparison easier, transparent, and source-linked, so users can form their own view with less noise and more evidence.

How To Use The Compass

  • Select an election (state-level or constituency-level compass).
  • Answer each policy thesis with approve, neutral, or reject.
  • Mark issue importance where available.
  • Review match output and compare explanations with source links.

What This Site Is For

  • Policy literacy before voting.
  • Quick side-by-side comparison of public stances.
  • Traceable public-source evidence for mapped positions.
  • Open contribution and correction workflow for source-backed updates.

What This Site Is Not

  • Not a voting recommendation engine.
  • Not affiliated with any party, alliance, candidate, or government.
  • Not a replacement for reading full manifestos and official statements.

Transparency and Privacy

Compass responses are processed in-browser for result calculation. The project does not intend to collect or sell personal response data.

For evidence links and source methodology, see Methodology & Sources.

Open Source and Contributions

The project is maintained openly on GitHub. If you find a mismatch, share a correction with documentary evidence.
